Carrots

Preferred warmth: 10°C - 23°C











What you’ll need

  • An elongated container, like a window box container, which is ideally 12 to 24 inches in diameter, 12 inches deep with several potting holes.
  • Carrot seeds from a small variety. Some of these are Parisian Heirloom, Danvers, Little Finger, Red Core Chantenay and Royal Chantenay.










Steps for planting

  1. Find a spot in your home which has 6 - 8 hours of sun daily with some shade in the afternoon.
  2. Fill your chosen container with loose well-draining soil. Make sure to leave no rocks or hard soil when potting or carrots will grow deformed.
  3. Plant seeds about ¼ of an inch below the soil around 3 inches apart.
  4. Cover the seeds slightly with moist Coconut Coir or wood shavings or compost to retain moisture.










Tips for growing

  • Water your carrots at least one inch of water per week. Make sure to keep the soil moist for seeds to germinate.
  • Keep them cool during summer since temperatures above 29 degrees will result in poorly grown and discoloured carrots.
  • Avoid repotting. This will slow their growth and might damage the plant.

The seeds should germinate within 2 weeks (10-21 days). They will be ready for harvest around 2 months (50- 75 days).
